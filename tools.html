The Tool Stack

Four production categories, ten tools each, with every pricing tier laid out. A tool tagged with a bubble shows up under more than one tab because its feature set genuinely spans both categories — that's not a mistake, it's how the market actually overlaps right now.

Read this before you subscribe to anything

Pricing on every tool below changes often — promo discounts, credit-system overhauls, and full pricing-page restructures are common in this market, and at least one widely-recommended tool from 2025 (PlayHT) was discontinued entirely after an acquisition in late 2025. Treat every number on this page as directionally accurate as of mid-2026, not as a live quote. Always confirm current pricing on the provider's own site before paying. Voice cloning specifically carries a consent requirement on every reputable platform — you generally need to either clone your own voice or have explicit written consent from whoever you're cloning.

For cloning your own voice (with your own consent, on yourself) to use in AI-narrated audio — plus the audio-editing tools that bundle cloning in as one feature among several.

Text-to-speech and AI narration tools built for shipping finished voiceovers — distinct from the cloning tab in that most of these lean on a library of pre-built voices rather than (or in addition to) cloning yours.

Text/image-to-video generators and AI avatar platforms. These create footage or presenters — they don't replace a timeline editor for assembling a finished video (see the Video Editing tab for that).

Which one should I actually pick?
If you want an AI presenter speaking your script (no camera needed) Pick HeyGen Creator (~$24–29/mo) — unlimited videos, easiest script-to-avatar workflow
If this is for enterprise training/onboarding at scale Pick Synthesia Creator (~$64–89/mo) — deepest template library, custom personal avatars
If you need cinematic B-roll or scene footage from a text prompt Pick Runway Standard (~$15/mo) or Kling Standard (~$8/mo) for the budget option
If you want the single highest visual quality, budget aside Pick Google Veo (via Google AI Pro, ~$28.99/mo) — strongest prompt adherence and native audio
If you're testing ideas before committing to a paid plan Pick HeyGen Free (3 videos/mo) or Pika Free — both give a real feel for output quality
If you want stylized/artistic clips rather than photorealism Pick Pika (~$10/mo) — built for creative effects over realism
A note on availability

OpenAI's standalone Sora web/app experience was discontinued in April 2026 (API access was slated to follow later in the year), and PlayHT was shut down in December 2025 after its acquisition by Meta. Both are omitted below for that reason — if you see them recommended in older articles, check current availability before planning a workflow around either.

Two different jobs, easy to conflate: HeyGen and Synthesia generate a presenter speaking your script — the closest fit if "AI video" means a talking-head explainer. Runway, Kling, Veo, Pika, and Luma generate scene footage from a prompt — the right tools for B-roll or stylized cutaways, not a presenter. Most production pipelines that use both pick one avatar tool and one or two generative tools rather than overlapping five.
